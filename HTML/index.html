BArts

*

BA (Bachelor of Arts) is a versatile undergraduate degree typically lasting three to four years, covering a broad range of liberal arts subjects, including humanities, social sciences, and fine arts. It emphasizes critical thinking, communication skills, and cultural understanding, often allowing students to choose a major or concentration in a specific field of study. BA programs may require a combination of coursework, research, and electives, and they vary by institution and country.

B-Tech

*

B.Tech (Bachelor of Technology) is a four-year undergraduate degree program in engineering and technology. It typically includes a blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills, with a focus on various engineering disciplines such as civil, mechanical, electrical, computer science, and more. B.Tech programs often require students to complete a final-year project or internship, and graduates can pursue careers in engineering, research, or technology-related fields.

BBA

*

BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration) is a 3 to 4-year undergraduate program that provides a comprehensive understanding of business principles, management, and leadership. It typically covers subjects like accounting, marketing, finance, and organizational behavior, preparing students for careers in various business sectors. BBA programs often include internships or practical experience to enhance real-world skills.

B.Com

*

B.Com (Bachelor of Commerce) is a 3-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on various aspects of business, finance, and economics. Students typically study subjects such as accounting, taxation, economics, and management. B.Com graduates often pursue careers in finance, accounting, marketing, and business management.

B.Sc

*

B.Sc (Bachelor of Science) in IT is basically about storing, processing, securing, and managing information. This degree is primarily focused on subjects such as software, databases, and networking.

MBBS

*

A MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) is a professional undergraduate degree program that typically spans 5-6 years, training students to become medical doctors. It includes both theoretical coursework and clinical rotations, focusing on medical science and patient care. Upon completion, graduates are eligible to practice medicine.
